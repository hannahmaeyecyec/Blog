Mt.Dulang-dulang (Mount Katanglad)

Lantapan, Bukidnon

By Hannah Yecyec | November 14, 2025

About

Mt. Dulang-Dulang, part of the majestic Mount Kitanglad Mountain Range, is one of the highest and most enchanting peaks in the Philippines. Known for its mossy forests, towering ancient trees, and cool misty atmosphere, the mountain offers hikers a magical experience that feels like walking through a living forest kingdom. Its rich biodiversity and serene environment make it a favorite destination for adventurers and nature lovers.

Beyond its natural beauty, Mt. Dulang-Dulang holds deep cultural and spiritual value. It is regarded as sacred by the Talaandig tribe, who perform rituals before each climb to honor the spirits of the mountain. Safety

This is a high-elevation, challenging trek, so physical readiness is crucial. Hike with a local guide and in groups to ensure safety and proper navigation. Watch for steep inclines, loose rocks, and unpredictable weather, and carry adequate water and first-aid supplies. Layered clothing is recommended for cold conditions, and be prepared to adjust your pace to avoid exhaustion. Always follow park rules and preserve the natural environment.

Trail

Mt. Dulang-Dulang begins in Kitanglad Range Natural Park. Hikers traverse dense mossy forests, rocky paths, and steep inclines, making the trek challenging and rewarding. Along the way, misty highland views, waterfalls, and diverse flora and fauna make the climb an unforgettable adventure. Proper hiking gear, physical preparation, and a local guide are highly recommended for a safe journey to the summit.

Weather Considerations

As a high-elevation peak, Mt. Dulang-Dulang can be cold, especially before sunrise and near the summit. Layered clothing is essential, and hikers should be prepared for mist or sudden rain. Check the weather forecast in advance, and avoid hiking during heavy rain to prevent slippery and dangerous trails.
