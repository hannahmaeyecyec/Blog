About

High above the lush valleys of Bukidnon, where clouds drift over ancient forests, lies Mount Tuminungan. Unlike the more famous peaks of Mindanao, Tuminungan offers adventurers a quiet, unspoiled escape into nature. What to See and Experience in Mt. Tuminungan

  • Breathtaking Panoramas

    The ridges of Mt. Tuminungan offer sweeping views of the Kitanglad Mountain Range, valleys, and drifting clouds. Perfect for photography or simply enjoying the scenery.

  • Cultural Connection

    Guides may perform rituals before hiking, reflecting the sacred traditions of local indigenous communities.

How to Get There:

  • Gateway City: Fly into Cagayan de Oro.
  • Travel to Bukidnon: Take a bus to Malaybalay City, then local transport to trailheads.
  • Trails to Tuminungan: Intavas, Impasug-ong, Lupiagan, Sumilao, Bol-ogan / Songco / Kaatoan, Lantapan (Cawayan Trail)

Why Visit Mt. Tuminungan

Though less famous, Mt. Tuminungan is a hidden gem. Its untouched trails, breathtaking views, and cultural heritage make it ideal for hikers seeking adventure and reflection. Visiting responsibly helps protect the ecosystem and supports indigenous communities.
