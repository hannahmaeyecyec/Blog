Preparation

Start early to enjoy cooler temperatures and catch the sunrise along the trail. Bring at least 2 liters of water and light snacks to keep your energy up. Wear comfortable clothing and sturdy hiking shoes, and let someone know your hiking plan. Decide whether to take the easier Sitio Capistrano route or the steeper Sitio Binalbagan path.

Gear Checklist

  • Lightweight hiking shoes
  • Comfortable clothing and hat
  • Backpack with 2L water and snacks
  • Sunscreen and sunglasses
  • Trekking poles for rocky sections
  • Camera for scenic views

Safety

Mt. Capistranoâ€™s steep and rocky trail demands steady footing, so wearing reliable hiking shoes is essential. Stay cautious on the sharp limestone sections, especially when the rocks are wet and slippery. Bring enough water, pace yourself on the inclines, and avoid climbing during heavy rain to prevent accidents. Hiring a local guide is also recommended for first-timers to ensure a safe and smooth hike.

Trail

The easier trail to Mt. Capistrano starts in Sitio Capistrano, offering a less crowded and more relaxed hike to the summit. The usual route from Sitio Binalbagan is steeper and more challenging, recommended for those seeking a tougher adventure. For hikers aiming to experience the mountain without the difficulty of the main trail, the Capistrano route provides a scenic and enjoyable ascent. Motorcycle riders can drop adventurers directly at the chosen starting point to begin the trek smoothly.

Weather Considerations

Mt. Capistrano enjoys mild temperatures, making early mornings and late afternoons ideal for hiking. Afternoon rains can occur, so a light raincoat or poncho is recommended. Clear skies offer the best views, so plan your trek during the dry season for safety and scenic vistas.
